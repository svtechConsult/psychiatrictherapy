Unlock Your Potential: ADHD Therapy for Adults in Columbus, OH

  • Dave
  • Jan 1, 2022

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder, commonly known as ADHD, is a neurodevelopmental disorder that affects individuals across their lifespan. Although ADHD is typically associated with children and adolescents, it can also be seen in adults. ADHD is characterized by difficulties with attention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity. Adults with ADHD may struggle in their personal and professional lives, as their symptoms can interfere with their ability to focus, organize, and prioritize tasks.

What is ADHD?

Understanding Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurological condition that manifests as a persistent pattern of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that is more frequent and severe than what is typically observed in individuals at a comparable level of development.

Types of ADHD

ADHD is classified into three subtypes: predominantly inattentive, predominantly hyperactive-impulsive, and combined. The symptoms of inattention include difficulty listening, forgetfulness, losing things, and becoming easily distracted. The symptoms of hyperactivity include fidgeting, restlessness, talking excessively, and difficulty engaging in quiet activities. The symptoms of impulsivity include blurting out answers, difficulty waiting for turns, and interrupting others.

Symptoms of ADHD

The symptoms of ADHD may vary from person to person, but common symptoms include difficulty focusing, forgetfulness, disorganization, excessive talking, impulsivity, and hyperactivity. ADHD can negatively impact academic and professional performance, as well as affect relationships with family and friends. While ADHD is often diagnosed in childhood, many individuals with ADHD are not diagnosed until adulthood.

ADHD is among the most common mental disorders in children and teens, but plenty of adults have it too. It's estimated that adult ADHD affects more than 8 million adults (or up to 5% of Americans). Many of them don't even know it.

Diagnosing adult ADHD can be challenging, as many of the symptoms overlap with other mental health conditions. However, common signs and symptoms of adult ADHD include difficulty with time management, distractibility, poor listening skills, impulsivity, and trouble with organization.

ADHD Assessment and Evaluation in Columbus, Ohio

Columbus, Ohio, has many healthcare professionals and clinics that specialize in the assessment and treatment of ADHD in adults. Those seeking diagnosis and evaluation should schedule an appointment with a qualified professional who can assess their symptoms and provide appropriate treatment recommendations.

Treatment Options for Adult ADHD

Medication Treatment for ADHD

While medication is not always necessary for adults with ADHD, it can be an effective medication management treatment option for those whose symptoms interfere significantly with their daily life. Stimulant medications such as Ritalin and Adderall can improve attention, increase focus, and reduce impulsivity. Non-stimulant medications such as Strattera can also be effective without the risk of addiction or abuse.

Behavioral Therapy for ADHD

Behavioral therapy can help adults with ADHD learn strategies for managing symptoms and improving daily functioning. Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) can help individuals learn how to recognize and challenge negative patterns of behavior and thought, which can help reduce symptoms and improve overall functioning.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for ADHD

Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is a type of psychotherapy that focuses on changing negative patterns of thinking and behavior. CBT can be an effective treatment option for adults with ADHD, as it can help improve coping skills, increase self-awareness, and reduce symptoms.

ADHD and Substance Abuse

ADHD is considered part of the neurodiversity spectrum, which recognizes that neurological differences are a natural part of human variation and should be respected and accommodated.

Living with ADHD

ADHD in Relationships and Work

ADHD can affect personal and professional relationships, as well as make it difficult to succeed in the workplace. However, with proper treatment and support, individuals with ADHD can learn effective coping mechanisms and strategies to help improve their relationships and job performance. Communication, time-management skills, and organization are key areas where individuals with ADHD can focus to improve their functioning.

Individuals with ADHD are at an increased risk of developing substance abuse disorders. They may turn to drugs or alcohol as a way to cope with the symptoms of ADHD. It is crucial for individuals with ADHD to seek appropriate treatment and support to reduce their risk of substance abuse disorders.

ADHD and Coexisting Conditions like Anxiety and Depression

Individuals with ADHD may also experience coexisting mental health conditions such as anxiety or depression. Treatment for ADHD may involve addressing these underlying mental health issues. Behavioral therapy and medication can be effective treatment options for comorbid mental health conditions.
